Business Formation in Spain: Why Spain Is an Attractive Country for Entrepreneurs

In this FAQ section by Accompany we explain how to start a business in Spain step by step, covering the key legal, tax and administrative requirements for business formation in Spain. Whether you’re a seasoned entrepreneur or launching your first project, opening a business in Spain as a foreigner can be complex without expert guidance.

From understanding company registration in Spain to choosing the right legal structure and complying with local regulations, knowing the requirements for starting a business in Spain is essential to ensure a smooth and successful market entry.

Advantages of a Company in Spain

Strategic Location and Connectivity

Spain is strategically located at the crossroads of Europe, Africa, and America, making it an ideal location for international trade. The country boasts excellent infrastructure and connectivity, including modern airports, ports, and a well-developed road network.

Tax Benefits

Spain offers attractive tax benefits for companies. It has a competitive corporate tax rate and various incentive measures for investments, including tax benefits for innovation, impatriates and certain sectors and regions.

Access to European Market

As a member of the European Union, Spain provides access to the large European market, with over 500 million consumers. This facilitates business within the EU and offers opportunities for growth and expansion.

Talent Pool and Labor Market

Spain boasts a well-educated workforce and a diverse talent pool, with skilled individuals in various sectors, including technology, tourism, and business services. Additionally, labor costs in Spain are generally lower than in other European countries.

Quality of Life

Spain is renowned for its high quality of life, with a pleasant climate, stunning landscapes, and a relaxed lifestyle. This makes it attractive for both entrepreneurs and employees seeking a balance between work and personal life.

Innovation and Research

Spain is increasingly investing in research and innovation, with numerous research centers, universities, and technology parks. This provides companies with the opportunity to collaborate with leading institutions and benefit from cutting-edge technologies.

Cultural Diversity and Business Opportunities

Spain has a rich cultural history and offers a range of business opportunities in various sectors, including tourism, gastronomy, fashion, and renewable energy. The country attracts millions of tourists and investors annually, providing new opportunities for growth and development.

Steps to Establish a Company in Spain

Step Phase Description Key Deliverable
01 Tax ID (NIE / NIF) Obtain the required tax identification numbers for shareholders and the company to operate legally in Spain. Valid NIE (individuals) or NIF (entities).
02 Structure & Governance Define the legal structure (e.g., SL, branch, subsidiary) and the management model that best fits your business goals. Defined Legal Entity & Governance Model.
03 Name & Brand Reserve the company name with the Trade Register and consider trademark and domain registration. Certificate of Denomination (RMC).
04 Bylaws & Statutes Draft the company statutes, bylaws, and deed of incorporation, including capital, shareholders, and governance rules. Signed Articles of Association.
05 Public Notary Sign the deed of incorporation before a Spanish notary, in person or via power of attorney. Public Deed of Incorporation.
06 Trade Register Obtain the company’s fiscal identification number and complete company registration in Spain. Official NIF & Registration Entry.
07 Banking Setup Open a Spanish business bank account to activate operations and manage company funds. Active Corporate IBAN.
08 Tax & Digital ID Request a digital certificate and register with the tax agency to start tax compliance. Digital Certificate & Form 036/037.
09 Social Security Obtain an employer social security number if hiring employees in Spain. Social Security Employer Code (CCC).

Branch Office, Subsidiary or Acquisition

When planning to open a branch or subsidiary, consider the differences between them:

Share Capital:

Opening a branch does not require capital, while a subsidiary needs €1 to €60,000, depending on the type.

Responsibility:

The parent company is fully responsible for a branch, while the officers and administrators of a subsidiary have legal and fiduciary responsibilities.

Management and Shareholders:

The organization differs for the two types, a Branch Office is ultimately just a representation of the parent company without a separate legal entity, board of directors or shareholders.

Taxation:

A branch pays taxes as a non-resident legal entity, while a subsidiary declares taxes as a Spanish company. Tax advantages are available for resident companies but not for non resident entities.

Consulting with professionals can help avoid difficulties in opening your company and fulfilling accounting and taxation requirements.
